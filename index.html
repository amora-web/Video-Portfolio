New Media Webtext Experiment

I combined two ideas from the composition two reaedings for my experiment. Although the main theme is about "Pop Culture." As you scroll there is no foundation to what is said on the page. In other words there is no "center". I like Derrida's idea of no "center" because having a clear structure does not allow for the "free play" of elements. I took this idea and mismatched elements, as well as different texts to create my own meaning of art. This whole project was an abstract idea for me at first but I read both readings and utilized them to help me figure out how I wanted to structure my "no structure" new media webtext.

Webtext Design

In this finial Webtext experiment, I learned how to make a multipage interactive experience. I have never coded before since this class, so creating an interactive idea that is weird where we become the controller or the "cyborg" of the webpage, was a risk for me. I was stepping out of my creative comfort zone when creating this webtext. The theme even came from a dream I had one night about the old game Oregon Trail. Creating a abstract project like this one was a fully vulnerable expierence for me because it was new and I had to give myself my own direction.

Course Goals


1. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

Over the course of learning and creating the three webtexts I had a lot of time to discover on my own the way technology plays into the art of making. The overall idea that I found is that techonology and coding can extend my human mind. For example in the third webtext experiment that was multipage, I had this thought and idea of coding a interactive game similar to the online game Oregon Trail. In the reading Cybrog Manifesto there is this idea where we are a hybrid of a human, mahcine, and animal. It ultimatley ends up being a metaphor for modern idenity. In my interactive game I extended a silly idea in my head using code and techonology. On top of that idea, in my interactive website, the controller/human is using techonology/machine to make the animal's descions. In this interactive game we are creating an idenity for the controller, the animal in the game, and an idenity for techonology. I would have never been able to make this idea without learning about code and how all techonology is essentially built on code.

2. Embrace experimentation

I have never coded before until I started this course. I have been expieimenting with every project I worked on in this class. This concept was very scary for me. Having to try something ne was very scary for me. I had to do things I've never done before and submit it for a grade. This taught me to take a step back and realize that my grades are not everything in education. I am learning something super cool. This feeling was an internal expierment for myslef. in relation to the compositions, I was trying a lot of different tactics and tools. In the second composition I attempted to connect the theme of my project to the Derrida Structure, Sign, and Play reading. The main idea from this reading that I took was that there is no center to stucture. The reading connects this idea to Pop Culture. I made a website about the history of Pop Culutre because I like the topic. As you scroll down you will see the content of the webtext gets less and less structured to show how the center is dissolving. This also supports Derridas question of what does anything mean, or what is a structure made up of?

3.Engage with others in a workshop environment

I have never had a problem with public speaking. I love talking and colaborating wit other peers. I think it is an effective tool to get constructive feedback on work as well as helping me figure out any issues I may have run into during the creation process. During our workshops I often would consult with peers about my work asking for their opinions and advice. I found I got the best feedback when I volunteered to present my project in front of the class. On compositon 3 I got up and explained my multipage webtext expierience. One of the most helpful pieces of feedback I recieved was to connect my project back to the reading for that week a little more. I have since revisted my project and discovered how my project connects to the Cyborg Manifesto reading. I hope to conitnue to share my work throughout the semester with peers in class because I want to continue to recieve feedback that will only enhance my creations.

4. Practice revision within and across media forms

Over the course of my acadmeic career since I was in elementary school, it used to be hard for me to take feedback and revise my work. It wasn't until High School where I learned that feedback and revison are crucial steps in putting out the best possible versions of my work. This semester I was able to continue to master the skill of recieinvg constructive criticism and using it to revise my webtext assignments. The most prominent example of me revising my work is after the second composition assignment. My original webtext idea for New Media was not going to relate to the Derrida reading at all. I was planning on making a research website about the music industry because I didn't know what else to do. I simply chose that theme because I like learning about anything in relation to music. I asked my classmate Braelyn to look at my project before I submitted it and she reccomended I try and make the webtext more centered around Derrida's ideas. I was disapointed at first because I was going to have to change all the work I had finished, but then I remembered how the reading talked about Pop Art. I also happen to really like talking about Pop Art, I love going to the Andy Worhol Museum in Pittsburgh. By revising the entire theme of the webtext I was easily able to connect one of Derrida's key ideas, no center to sturcture, to my webtext. This was a large revison but I feel that it was a sucessful revision that made my webtext more well rounded in relation to the assignment and readings.

5. Learn to present your work

I personally love public speaking. I love to speak with new peers and people. I think it is one of the most effective ways to share and revise ideas or work. For all three webtext projects I have volunteered to share my work in front of the class. One thing I had to work on when presenting to the class is my time management. Because I love to talk, I tend to not keep track of time when I am presenting. For example during the first Webtext presentation, Narrative about me, we had two minutes to go through and introduce ourselves and our epierences with technology. I went over the time limit because it was my first time having a time limit. I wanted to get better at managing the time of a presentation better. On the third compositon I volunteered to present my project. I managed to get through the main points of my multipage experiment in a timely manner. This left enough time to recivie feedback from the professor and fellow peers. Presenting my work has become one of the most helpful skills I have learned and want to continue to improve on.
